Privacy Policy

Effective Date: August 6, 2025

Last Updated: August 6, 2025

1. Introduction

MLB Sun Tracker ("we," "our," or "the Service") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our web application.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

We collect information you voluntarily provide when using the Service:

  • Preferences: Stadium selections, favorite teams, seating preferences
  • Location Data: ZIP code or city for weather and venue recommendations (if provided)
  • Game Selections: Dates, times, and venues you search for
  • Filter Settings: Sun exposure preferences, price ranges, seating areas

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you use the Service, we automatically collect:

  • Device Information: Browser type, operating system, device type (mobile/desktop)
  • Usage Data: Pages viewed, features used, time spent on pages
  • Performance Data: Load times, error logs, feature performance
  • IP Address: For geographic region detection (not stored permanently)

2.3 Local Storage

We use browser local storage to save:

  • Theme preferences (light/dark mode)
  • Recently viewed stadiums
  • Filter preferences
  • Language settings

3. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information to:

  • Provide sun exposure calculations and shade recommendations
  • Display relevant weather forecasts
  • Remember your preferences for future visits
  • Improve Service performance and features
  • Generate anonymous usage statistics
  • Troubleshoot technical issues
  • Comply with legal obligations

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

4.1 We Do NOT:

  • Sell your personal information
  • Share your data with advertisers
  • Track you across other websites
  • Create user profiles for marketing

4.2 We May Share Information:

  • Service Providers: With trusted third-party services that help operate the Service (e.g., hosting, analytics)
  • Legal Requirements: If required by law or to protect rights and safety
  • Business Transfers: In connection with a merger or acquisition
  • Aggregated Data: Anonymous, aggregated statistics that cannot identify individuals

6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

6.1 Essential Cookies

We use essential cookies for:

  • Session management
  • Security features
  • Load balancing

6.2 Functional Cookies

Optional cookies that enhance functionality:

  • Remember preferences
  • Language settings
  • Theme selection

6.3 Analytics

We use privacy-focused analytics that:

  • Do not use cookies
  • Do not track individuals
  • Aggregate data only

7. Data Security

We implement security measures including:

  • HTTPS encryption for all data transmission
  • Regular security updates and patches
  • Limited access to user data
  • No storage of sensitive personal information
  • Regular security audits

8. Data Retention

  • Local Storage: Retained until you clear browser data
  • Server Logs: Automatically deleted after 30 days
  • Analytics Data: Aggregated and anonymized immediately
  • No Account Data: We don't require accounts, so no persistent user data

9. Your Privacy Rights

9.1 You Have the Right to:

  • Access: Request information about data we have
  • Delete: Clear all local storage data
  • Opt-Out: Disable analytics and non-essential features
  • Portability: Export your preferences
  • Correction: Update any stored preferences

9.2 How to Exercise Rights:

  • Clear browser data to remove all local storage
  • Use browser privacy modes for anonymous browsing
  • Disable JavaScript to prevent data collection
  • Contact us for specific requests

9.3 GDPR Rights (European Users)

If you are in the European Economic Area, you have additional rights under GDPR:

  • Right to be informed about data processing
  • Right to rectification of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to processing
  • Rights related to automated decision-making

10. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

California residents have specific rights under CCPA:

  • Right to know what personal information is collected
  • Right to know if personal information is sold or disclosed
  • Right to say no to the sale of personal information
  • Right to access personal information
  • Right to equal service and price

Note: We do not sell personal information.

11. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have collected information from a child under 13, please contact us immediately.

12. International Data Transfers

The Service is hosted in the United States. If you access the Service from outside the United States, your information may be transferred to and processed in the United States.

13. Do Not Track Signals

The Service responds to Do Not Track (DNT) browser signals. When DNT is enabled, we disable analytics and non-essential features.

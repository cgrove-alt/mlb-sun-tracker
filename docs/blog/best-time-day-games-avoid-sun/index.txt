2c:T1a5a,<h2>Understanding Sun Patterns at MLB Stadiums</h2>
<p>The sun&#39;s position dramatically affects shade coverage at baseball stadiums. A 1:00 PM game offers completely different shade patterns than a 4:00 PM start. Understanding these patterns can help you choose the best game times for comfort.</p>
<h2>Early Afternoon Games (12:00-1:00 PM Start)</h2>
<h3>Pros</h3>
<ul>
<li>Third base side typically gets shade first</li>
<li>Upper deck overhangs provide maximum coverage</li>
<li>Sun is high, creating more natural shade</li>
</ul>
<h3>Cons</h3>
<ul>
<li>Peak UV exposure time</li>
<li>Field level seats get minimal shade</li>
<li>Outfield bleachers are completely exposed</li>
</ul>
<h3>Best Stadiums for Early Games</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>Yankee Stadium</strong> - Extensive upper deck coverage</li>
<li><strong>Fenway Park</strong> - Green Monster provides unique shade</li>
<li><strong>Progressive Field</strong> - Well-designed overhangs</li>
</ol>
<h2>Mid-Afternoon Games (3:00-4:00 PM Start)</h2>
<h3>Pros</h3>
<ul>
<li>Sun begins moving west, creating more shade</li>
<li>First base side starts getting coverage</li>
<li>Temperature typically cooler than noon</li>
</ul>
<h3>Cons</h3>
<ul>
<li>Sun glare can affect viewing angles</li>
<li>Shade patterns shift during the game</li>
<li>Some sections transition from shade to sun</li>
</ul>
<h3>Ideal Seating Areas</h3>
<ul>
<li>Sections behind home plate</li>
<li>Third base side (especially upper levels)</li>
<li>Club level with overhang protection</li>
</ul>
<h2>Late Afternoon Games (5:00-7:00 PM Start)</h2>
<h3>Pros</h3>
<ul>
<li>Most shade coverage of any day game</li>
<li>Cooler temperatures</li>
<li>Sun sets during later innings</li>
</ul>
<h3>Cons</h3>
<ul>
<li>Sun glare in early innings</li>
<li>Shadows can affect field visibility</li>
<li>Rush hour traffic considerations</li>
</ul>
<h2>Seasonal Considerations</h2>
<h3>Spring (April-May)</h3>
<p><strong>Best Times</strong>: 3:00-4:00 PM starts</p>
<ul>
<li>Lower sun angle creates more shade</li>
<li>Cooler temperatures reduce heat stress</li>
<li>Third base side ideal for afternoon games</li>
</ul>
<h3>Summer (June-August)</h3>
<p><strong>Best Times</strong>: 5:00-7:00 PM starts</p>
<ul>
<li>Avoid peak heat hours</li>
<li>Maximum shade in later innings</li>
<li>Consider night games when possible</li>
</ul>
<h3>Fall (September-October)</h3>
<p><strong>Best Times</strong>: 1:00-2:00 PM starts</p>
<ul>
<li>Pleasant temperatures</li>
<li>Lower sun angle throughout</li>
<li>Earlier sunset times</li>
</ul>
<h2>Stadium-Specific Recommendations</h2>
<h3>East Coast Stadiums</h3>
<p><strong>Optimal Times</strong>: 4:00-7:00 PM</p>
<ul>
<li>Sun sets behind most stadiums</li>
<li>Later starts avoid peak heat</li>
<li>Examples: Yankee Stadium, Fenway Park, Citizens Bank Park</li>
</ul>
<h3>West Coast Stadiums</h3>
<p><strong>Optimal Times</strong>: 1:00-4:00 PM</p>
<ul>
<li>Marine layer provides natural cooling</li>
<li>Sun angle favors afternoon games</li>
<li>Examples: Oracle Park, Petco Park, Dodger Stadium</li>
</ul>
<h3>Midwest Stadiums</h3>
<p><strong>Optimal Times</strong>: 3:00-5:00 PM</p>
<ul>
<li>Balance between heat and shade</li>
<li>Variable weather considerations</li>
<li>Examples: Wrigley Field, Busch Stadium, Target Field</li>
</ul>
<h2>Tips for Different Start Times</h2>
<h3>1:00 PM Games</h3>
<ul>
<li>Arrive early to find shaded standing areas</li>
<li>Bring maximum sun protection</li>
<li>Consider upper deck seats</li>
<li>Hydrate extensively before the game</li>
</ul>
<h3>4:00 PM Games</h3>
<ul>
<li>Check sunset time for your date</li>
<li>First base side gets late-game shade</li>
<li>Bring sunglasses for sun glare</li>
<li>Dress in layers for temperature changes</li>
</ul>
<h3>7:00 PM Games</h3>
<ul>
<li>Still considered &quot;day games&quot; in summer</li>
<li>Sun sets during middle innings</li>
<li>West-facing seats get direct sun early</li>
<li>Best overall comfort level</li>
</ul>
<h2>Special Considerations</h2>
<h3>Getaway Day Games</h3>
<p>Many Thursday afternoon games start at 12:35 PM</p>
<ul>
<li>Maximum sun exposure</li>
<li>Prioritize covered seating</li>
<li>Consider weeknight games instead</li>
</ul>
<h3>Weekend Day Games</h3>
<p>Saturday/Sunday games typically 1:00-4:00 PM</p>
<ul>
<li>Most crowded shade sections</li>
<li>Arrive early for best spots</li>
<li>Premium shaded seats sell out first</li>
</ul>
<h3>Holiday Games</h3>
<p>July 4th, Memorial Day, Labor Day often day games</p>
<ul>
<li>Peak attendance and sun exposure</li>
<li>Book shaded seats well in advance</li>
<li>Consider special ticket packages with club access</li>
</ul>

<h2>Shade Hacks by Start Time</h2>
<h3>Morning Games (11:00 AM - 12:30 PM)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Sit as high as possible</li>
<li>West side of stadium best</li>
<li>Bring portable shade</li>
</ul>
<h3>Afternoon Games (1:00 PM - 3:30 PM)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Third base/left field optimal</li>
<li>Seek structural overhangs</li>
<li>Club level worth the cost</li>
</ul>
<h3>Evening Games (4:00 PM - 7:00 PM)</h3>
<ul>
<li>First base side for late shade</li>
<li>Behind home plate safest bet</li>
<li>Lower levels become viable</li>
</ul>
<h2>Conclusion</h2>
